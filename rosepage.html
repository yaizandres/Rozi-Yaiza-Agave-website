To pink or not to pink? ―

Colour is all about timinig! Rosé is made when the skins of red grapes touch wine for only a short time. As opposed to red wines, which macerate for weeks in contact with red grape skins, rosé wines are stained for just a few hours. So any red wine grape (from Cabernet Sauvignon to Syrah) can be used to make rosé wine!

Rosé, rosato & rosado »

Rosé- the real OG ―

Despite its current popularity amongst millennials, the first ever wine made was probably most similar in colour to a rosé! This is because thousands of years ago, before the advanced winemaking techniques, the grapes were pressed soon after harnesting by feet, creating juice that was only lightly pigmented.

(Most) Rosés don't improve with age―

Rosés should be sipped within one year of bottling! And importantly, you can drink it all year around... break the rules. It is not just a summer wine!
