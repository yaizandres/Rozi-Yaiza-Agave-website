Chardonnay

Dry. Rich, creamy, nutty. Lemon, grapefruit, vanilla and toffee. Fish and chicken dishes.

Sauvignon Blanc

Dry. Light, herbal, grassy. Green fruits, black currant. Seafood, poultry, salads.

Sémillon

Dry. Full-bodied, floral. Figs, berries. Seafood.

Moscato

Sweet. Grapefruit, honey, musky. Dessert.

Sweet. Off-dry, apricots, peaches. Middle eastern and Morrocan cuisine.

Riesling

Sweet. Off-dry, fresh apples. Chinese food, Cajun cuisine, Tex-Mex, Thai cuisine.

Temperature, matters! ―

Lighter white wines should be serverd chilled (7-10 ̊C) to emphasise their acidic and refreshing qualities. Wines with more body, on the other hand, are best served less cold (10-13 ̊C) because their fruity aromas come out at the warmer end of the spectrum.

Did you know...?―

There are some Malvasía grape vineyards in the Canary Island of Lanzarote, which grow on small volcanic ash rock terrain called "picón" and don't need any watering. The vines are planted inside a funnel-shaped hole (5m wide, 3m deep) and covered with thick layers of picón. The porous volcanic granules retain the dew, providing in this way water for the vines. Also, a semicircular wall is built around the hole to protect the plant from strong winds.
